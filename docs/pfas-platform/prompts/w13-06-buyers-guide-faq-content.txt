### Tips for Success

**The Water Test:**
Heat your pan over medium heat. Flick water droplets on the surface:
- If they evaporate immediately: too hot
- If they sizzle and disappear in 1-2 seconds: too hot
- If they form beads that dance across the surface: perfect!

**Use enough fat:**
Don't be stingy with oil or butter. A thin, even layer helps prevent sticking.

**Let food release naturally:**
Food will release when it's ready. If it's sticking, it's not done searing.
Wait 30-60 seconds longer before trying to flip.

**Deglaze for easy cleanup:**
After cooking, add a splash of wine, broth, or water while the pan is hot. 
This creates a sauce AND makes cleanup easier.

### Seasoning 101

**What is seasoning?**
Seasoning is a layer of polymerized oil baked onto the iron surface. It 
creates a natural nonstick coating and protects against rust.

**How to maintain it:**
1. After cooking, rinse with hot water
2. Use a brush or scraper for stuck bits (soap is fine occasionally)
3. Dry immediately and thoroughly
4. Apply a thin layer of oil while warm
5. Heat briefly to evaporate excess

**How to restore:**
If your pan becomes rusty or loses its seasoning:
1. Scrub with steel wool to remove rust
2. Wash and dry completely
3. Apply thin layer of oil (flaxseed, vegetable, or cast iron conditioner)
4. Bake upside-down at 450Â°F for 1 hour
5. Repeat 2-3 times for best results

## What About "Ceramic" Nonstick?

You'll see many pans marketed as "ceramic" nonstick. Here's what you need 
to know:

### "Ceramic Coating" is NOT Ceramic

True ceramic is kiln-fired clay. "Ceramic coating" is a sol-gel (silica-based) 
coating applied at low temperatures. It's a different category entirely.

### Are Sol-Gel Coatings PFAS-Free?

**It depends.** Some brands (like GreenPan's Thermolon) claim to be PFAS-free. 
Others may use PTFE or other fluoropolymers as additives.

**We recommend:**
1. Check our verification tier for specific products
2. Look for brands that explicitly state "no PTFE" and "no PFAS"
3. Contact manufacturers directly if uncertain

### Sol-Gel Coating Limitations
- Shorter lifespan than stainless or cast iron (1-5 years typical)
- Nonstick properties degrade over time
- Not oven safe to high temperatures
- Can be damaged by metal utensils

### ðŸš© "Non-Toxic" Without Specifics

### ðŸš© "Green" or "Eco-Friendly" Coating
Doesn't mean PFAS-free. May still contain fluoropolymers.

### ðŸš© "Ceramic" Without Clarification

## Questions to Ask Before Buying
